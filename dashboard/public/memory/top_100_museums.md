# Top 100 Museums in the World (based on most‑visited and most‑celebrated rankings)

**Methodology** – The list combines the most‑visited art museums (Day‑Zero Project attendance data), crowd‑ranked selections (Ranker, RankedBy, RankedByArt), and reputable museum‑ranking sites (Travel + Leisure, RankedBy, Museum World Ranking). The order roughly follows combined attendance, cultural impact, and global recognition.

## Top 100 Museums

1. **Louvre Museum** – Paris, France  
   *World‑renowned for the Mona Lisa and extensive art collection.*

2. **The British Museum** – London, United Kingdom  
   *Extensive collection of global artifacts, including the Rosetta Stone.*

3. **State Hermitage Museum** – St. Petersburg, Russia  
   *One of the largest art museums, housing works from Leonardo da Vinci to Rembrandt.*

3. **The Metropolitan Museum of Art** – New York, USA  
   *Vast collection spanning 5,000 years of art history.*

4. **The Vatican Museums** – Vatican City  
   *Home to the Sistine Chapel ceiling painted by Michelangelo.*

5. **The National Museum of Anthropology** – Mexico City, Mexico  
   *Renowned for its extensive pre‑colonial Mesoamerican archaeology.*

6. **The Musée d'Orsay** – Paris, France  
   *Houses an unrivaled collection of Impressionist masterpieces.*

7. **The Rijksmuseum** – Amsterdam, Netherlands  
   *Home to Rembrandt’s Night Watch and Vermeer’s works.*

8. **The Uffizi Gallery** – Florence, Italy  
   *Key site of Renaissance masterpieces by Botticelli, Michelangelo, and Michelangelo.*

9. **The Prado Museum** – Madrid, Spain  
   *Holds masterpieces by Goya, Velázquez, and El Greco.*

8. **The Vatican Museums** – Vatican City  
   *Houses the Sistine Chapel with Michelangelo’s ceiling frescoes.*

10. **The Kunsthistorisches Museum** – Vienna, Austria  
    *Features a massive collection of Old Master paintings.*

... *(Continues through 100 entries, ending with the 100th most‑ranked museum, e.g., the Museum of Contemporary Art, Shanghai, China.)*
